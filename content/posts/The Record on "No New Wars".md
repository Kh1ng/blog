In response to Trump saying: "First of all, I didn't guarantee no war." and later in the same interview: "I didn't promise anything." I was again inspired to do my own research. What follows is a chronological record of what he said, when, and in what context. This is not a complete historical record, just quotes and dialog I found relevant and interesting. Sources were retrieved on or around the time of writing.

## 2016 Campaign

On **September 7, 2016** Trump described Clinton as "trigger happy and unstable when it comes to war", "reckless", and said her foreign policy brought "failure and death." He positioned his own approach as diplomatic rather than military. "Unlike my opponent, my foreign policy will emphasize diplomacy, not destruction. Hillary Clinton’s legacy in Iraq, Libya, and Syria has produced only turmoil and suffering. Her destructive policies have displaced millions of people, then she has invited the refugees into the West with no plan to screen them." This is not an explicit no new wars claim, but could be implied. [realclearpolitics.com](https://www.realclearpolitics.com/video/2016/09/07/trump_foreign_policy_speech_clinton_trigger_happy_unstable_and_reckless_she_brings_failure_and_death.html) has a the full marks prepared for delivery and the full speech is on [youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6BpuNbXDpoQ). 

More of the same from [Brietbart](https://www.breitbart.com/politics/2016/05/07/donald-trump-hillary-clinton-is-trigger-happy-on-foreign-policy/) on **May 7th, 2016** but with the addition of Trump's explicit claim he would never enter a bike race.

On **October 25, 2016** "You're going to end up in World War III over Syria if we listen to Hillary Clinton." is quoted verbatim across [CBS](https://www.cbsnews.com/amp/newyork/news/trump-clinton-syria-world-war-three/), [Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/hillary-clinton-would-start-world-war-iii-syria-donald-trump-513642), and [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2016/10/25/politics/donald-trump-hillary-clinton-world-war-iii-syria). Again, not an explicit no new wars claim but rather the opposite where he argued America's best efforts were spent pursuing ISIS as Clinton's proposed no-fly zone would put the US in direct conflict with Russia, a nuclear power.

## First Term (2017–2021)
On **January 3, 2020** while making remarks after the Soleimani strike at Mar-a-Lago. "We took action last night to stop a war. We did not take action to start a war. We do not seek regime change." From the [White House Archives](https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/briefings-statements/remarks-president-trump-killing-qasem-soleimani/) and [UVA Miller Center](https://millercenter.org/the-presidency/presidential-speeches/january-3-2020-remarks-killing-qasem-soleimani).

On **August 27, 2020** at the Republican National Convention acceptance speech, Trump again compared himself to other administrations. "Unlike previous administrations, I have kept America OUT of new wars – and our troops are coming home." and then discussed increased military spending and attacked Biden for having "sent their sons and daughters to fight in endless foreign wars."  Transcripts retrieved from [ABC](https://abcnews.com/Politics/full-text-donald-trumps-2020-republican-national-convention/story?id=72659782) and [NBC](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/2020-election/read-full-text-president-donald-trump-s-acceptance-speech-rnc-n1238636).

On **January 19, 2021** During his farewell address. Trump said, "I am especially proud to be the first President in decades who has started no new wars." ([Trump White House Archives](https://trumpwhitehouse.archives.gov/farewell-address/))

## 2024 Campaign

**January 2023** at New Hampshire campaign stops. "Through weakness and incompetence, Joe Biden has brought us to the brink of World War III. As president, I will bring back peace through strength", pulled from [NBC](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/donald-trump/trump-are-brink-world-war-iii-rcna68008). Trump then pledged to negotiate a Ukraine peace deal within 24 hours.

**March 13, 2023** at his Davenport, Iowa rally. "I am the only candidate who can make this promise: I will prevent World War III." Same speech: "Shortly after I win the presidency, I will have the disastrous war between Russia and Ukraine settled. It will take 24 hours." Straight from [Brietbart]([https://www.newsmax.com/us/world-war-iii-donald-trump-russia/2023/03/13/id/1112113/](https://www.breitbart.com/politics/2023/03/14/donald-trump-iowa-i-am-only-candidate-who-can-prevent-wwiii/)) and [Newsmax](https://www.newsmax.com/us/world-war-iii-donald-trump-russia/2023/03/13/id/1112113/)

**May 10, 2023** CNN Town Hall, Goffstown, NH. When asked how he would end the Ukraine war: "If I'm president, I will have that war settled in one day, 24 hours." CNN's Daniel Dale later catalogued at least 53 instances of the 24-hour Ukraine pledge across 2023 and 2024. ([CNN, April 25, 2025](https://www.cnn.com/2025/04/25/politics/fact-check-trump-ukraine-war))

**June 27, 2024** At the CNN presidential debate vs. Biden in Atlanta. Trump said "These are wars that will never end with him. [Biden] will drive us into World War Three and we're closer to World War Three than anybody can imagine. We are very, very close to World War Three, and he's driving us there." On Ukraine: "I will have that war settled between Putin and Zelenskyy as president-elect before I take office on January 20th. I'll have that war settled." Retrieved from [The American Presidency Project](https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/presidential-debate-atlanta-georgia)

**July 18, 2024** RNC acceptance speech, Milwaukee. "I was the first president in modern times to start NO NEW WARS." He said the Russia-Ukraine war and the Hamas attack "would have never happened if I was president," and that "our opponents inherited a world at peace and turned it into a planet of war." He also said "I could stop wars with a telephone call." ([NBC Boston full transcript](https://www.nbcboston.com/news/national-international/read-donald-trumps-full-rnc-speech-accepting-gop-nomination/3432055/))

**September 10, 2024 — ABC presidential debate vs. Harris, Philadelphia.** On Ukraine: "If I were president it would have never started." On settlement: "If I'm President-Elect, I'll get it done before even becoming president." ([ABC News transcript](https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/harris-trump-presidential-debate-transcript/story?id=113560542))

**October 27, 2024 — Madison Square Garden rally, NYC.** "You're not going to have a war with me and you're not going to have a third world war with me, that I can tell you." [Snopes](https://www.snopes.com/fact-check/trump-video-no-war-with-iran/) verified the clip's authenticity. 

**November 6, 2024 — Victory speech, Palm Beach.** "We want a strong and powerful military. And ideally, we don't have to use it. You know, we had no war — four years, we had no wars, except we defeated ISIS. We defeated ISIS in record time, but we had no wars. They said: 'He will start a war.' I'm not going to start a war. I'm going to stop wars." [UCSB's American Presidency Project](https://www.presidency.ucsb.edu/documents/remarks-accepting-election-the-47th-president-the-united-states-palm-beach-florida)

## Second Term (2025–2026)

**January 20, 2025 — Second Inaugural Address.** "My proudest legacy will be that of a peacemaker and unifier." ([White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/remarks/2025/01/the-inaugural-address/))

**March - June 2025** Multiple instances. Ukraine pledge: "Well, I said that figuratively, and I said that as an exaggeration… it was said in jest." He separately described it as "a little bit sarcastic" and then "sarcastic" in other interviews. ([Time](https://time.com/7280114/donald-trump-2025-interview-transcript/)) ([The Hill](https://thehill.com/homenews/administration/5197961-trump-says-he-was-being-a-little-bit-sarcastic-when-he-promised-to-end-ukraine-war-in-24-hours/)) ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/08/15/politics/fact-check-trump-ukraine-war))

**Late 2025 — "Ended eight wars" claim.** Trump repeatedly posted on social media that he had "ended 8 wars in just 8 months." The claim was repeated in the December 2025 National Security Strategy. The eight conflicts listed included Israel-Hamas, Israel-Iran, India-Pakistan, Thailand-Cambodia, DRC-Rwanda, and Serbia-Kosovo, among others. Write up by BBC [here](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5y3599gx4qo)

At a press conference with German Chancellor Friedrich Merz, **March 3, 2026** on the US-Israel strikes on Iran (Operation Epic Fury). The operation killed Iranian Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Six US service members died in the ensuing conflict. "I might've forced their hand. It was my opinion that these lunatics were gonna attack first." [Time](https://time.com/7382322/trump-iran-war-progress-knocked-out-reason-for-strikes/)

**June 7, 2026 — NBC Meet the Press interview with Kristen Welker, taped at Custer Farms, Chippewa Falls, Wisconsin.** Welker: "One of your consistent campaign promises was no new wars, going all the way back to 2015. Did you break that promise to the American people?" Trump: "No." When pressed: "First of all, I didn't guarantee no war. Why would I have built the strongest military in the world?" When Welker noted he had made the claim "over and over again," Trump said: "I didn't promise anything. I don't like these endless wars. This is not an endless war." He called Welker "a big liberal" and ended the interview shortly after. [Mediaite](https://www.mediaite.com/media/news/did-you-break-that-promise-nbcs-kristen-welker-grills-trump-on-no-new-wars-pledge-president-snaps-in-response-calling-her-a-big-liberal/) and [CNN](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/donald-trump/read-transcript-president-donald-trump-interviewed-nbc-news-meet-press-rcna348508)
